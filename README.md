Business Analytics & Marketing Metrics – Learning Journey

This project is a complete walkthrough of my learning journey into the world of business analytics, data analytics, and marketing performance measurement. The goal of this assignment was to move beyond just understanding definitions and instead develop a practical, real-world understanding of how data is used to drive business decisions.

I started by exploring the foundations of data — understanding the difference between business intelligence, data analytics, and business analytics. This helped me see how organizations collect, store, and analyze data to gain insights. From there, I moved into data-driven decision-making, where I learned how companies rely on data instead of assumptions to guide their strategies.

As I progressed, I focused on key analytical concepts such as descriptive, diagnostic, predictive, and prescriptive analytics. These helped me understand how businesses not only analyze past performance but also explain why things happened, predict future outcomes, and recommend actions.

The assignment then shifted into business performance metrics, especially customer and sales KPIs. Here, I explored important concepts like Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC), Customer Lifetime Value (CLV), churn rate, and retention rate. One of the most important insights I gained was understanding the relationship between CLV and CAC, and how this ratio determines whether a business is truly profitable or just growing without sustainability.

In the final section, I focused on marketing analytics and campaign performance. I learned how businesses plan and execute marketing campaigns, measure their effectiveness using metrics like ROI and ROAS, and improve performance using tools like A/B testing. I also explored how companies track customer behavior through sales funnels and use marketing attribution to identify which channels drive results.

Overall, this project helped me connect theory with real-world application. It showed me how data flows through a business, from collection, to analysis, to decision-making, and how different metrics and tools work together to improve performance. This assignment is not just about definitions, but about understanding how modern businesses operate in a data-driven world.
